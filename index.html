How the numbers were measured — important caveats

  • Reach/impressions are not public on Instagram — only page admins see them in Insights. Likes, comments, and (for Reels) view counts are used as proxies here.
  • Post counts are sampled, not exhaustive. Several pages post in same-day batches (e.g. multiple district bulletins at once); counts shown are what was directly verified by opening posts, not a guaranteed total.
  • Comment counts weren't reliably extractable from the public UI for high-engagement posts (pagination hides most of them) — comments are treated qualitatively, not quantitatively, in this analysis.
  • Ad Library data is genuinely public and reliable — this is the most trustworthy dataset in this report.

What separates the high performers from the low performers

Across every single page in this audit, the same pattern holds. This is the clearest, most actionable signal in the whole dataset.

Real people, on camera, telling a personal story in their own words (a recovering addict, a farmer, a pilgrim, an elderly villager reminiscing) — these posts out-perform official/government-branded posts by 5–50x on every page that has both formats.

Text-heavy graphic templates — CM portrait + stat card + hashtags, with no human testimonial — reliably land in the single digits to low tens of likes, even on pages with 10K+ followers.

What "quality" actually means across this portfolio

Content quality is genuinely high where it's human

Production values are consistent — good camera work, clean graphics, official branding used correctly across every page. The gap between best and worst pages isn't a skills gap, it's a formula gap: pages that lean on testimonials score dramatically higher than pages that lean on stat cards.

Reach quality is a different story from content quality

Sehat Yojana has some of the best individual content (the 377-like child recovery story) but the worst engagement health overall, because heavy paid promotion is buying views that don't convert to interest. Content quality ≠ audience quality when reach is bought rather than earned.

That's worth a direct conversation with whoever manages the ad spend: is this intentional (the agency's pages perform better), or is it money going around the official channel rather than through it?

What's not going well

Gangstran Te Vaar's daily bulletins are dead weight

The district-wise static bulletins are getting 0–6 likes on a 15K-follower page. They add posting volume but not audience engagement — worth questioning whether that format should continue as-is, or be replaced with the higher-performing Reel format.

Village Playgrounds has quietly stopped making Reels

Its best-performing format (Reels, 2,500+ views) hasn't had a new entry in 3+ weeks. All current output is a repeating static template.

Compliance flags on multiple ad campaigns

Gangstran Te Vaar (2 ads) and Sehat Yojana (several ads) were flagged by Meta for running "without a required disclaimer." For official government-linked pages, that's a compliance risk worth fixing before the next boost.

Teerth Yatra's real distribution bypasses the official page

See the Ad Boost tab — ~73 ads running through proxy pages, none through the official handle. Whoever owns that ad budget should confirm this is deliberate strategy, not oversight.
